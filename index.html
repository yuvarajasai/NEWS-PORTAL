CSK won the toss and elected to bat first, posting a competitive total of 185/6 in their 20 overs. Opener Faf du Plessis top-scored with a brilliant knock of 89 off just 52 balls, while captain MS Dhoni played a captain's knock, scoring a quickfire 35 off 18 balls towards the end of the innings. In reply, Mumbai Indians got off to a poor start, losing opener Rohit Sharma in the first over itself. However, Suryakumar Yadav and Ishan Kishan stitched together a fine partnership to keep their team in the hunt. Imran Tahir and Ravindra Jadeja also chipped in with two wickets each. With this win, CSK have lifted their fourth IPL trophy, joining Mumbai Indians as the most successful team in the tournament's history. MS Dhoni, who had announced his retirement from international cricket earlier this year, was ecstatic after the win and credited his team's efforts for the victory. "This win is for all our fans who have supported us through thick and thin. It's been a tough journey, but we stuck together as a team and came out on top. Credit to the boys for the way they played throughout the tournament," Dhoni said in the post-match presentation ceremony. Meanwhile, Faf du Plessis, who was named the Player of the Match for his brilliant knock, said he was happy to contribute to the team's success. "It's a great feeling to contribute to the team's success. We knew it was going to be a tough game, but we stuck to our plans and executed them well. I'm glad I could play my part in the win," du Plessis said. The IPL 2023 season was played in a bio-secure bubble across multiple venues due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. The Beast Is Back......

Iconic Nissan GT-R R34 returns as limited-edition model After almost 20 years since its production ended, the Nissan GT-R R34 is back, at least for a select group of fans and collectors. Nissan has announced the release of a limited-edition version of the legendary sports car, which combines the classic design and performance of the original with modern upgrades and exclusivity. The Nissan GT-R R34, which was produced from 1999 to 2002, gained a cult following among enthusiasts for its distinctive look, advanced technology, and impressive performance on the track and the street. The car featured a twin-turbocharged 2.6-liter inline-six engine that produced up to 500 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, along with advanced features such as all-wheel drive, active aerodynamics, and a sophisticated suspension system. The new Nissan GT-R R34 limited edition pays homage to the original in several ways, including its signature Bayside Blue color, which was exclusive to the R34 in Japan. The car also features the same four round tail lights, hood vents, and rear spoiler as the classic model, as well as a unique badge and commemorative plaque that indicates the number of the car in the series. Under the hood, however, the new GT-R R34 has some notable improvements, such as a more powerful engine that delivers 600 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque, thanks to upgraded turbos, fuel injectors, and exhaust system. The car also benefits from a reinforced chassis, larger brakes, and a retuned suspension that allows for more precise handling and comfort. According to Nissan, only 50 units of the GT-R R34 limited edition will be produced, and each one will be customized to the owner's preferences. The car will be available in Japan, the United States, Canada, and several other markets, with a starting price of around $400,000. Deliveries are expected to begin in late 2023. "The Nissan GT-R R34 is a true icon of our brand and the automotive industry, and we're proud to bring it back to life for a lucky few who appreciate its legacy and performance," said Hiroshi Tamura, chief product specialist of the GT-R. "This limited edition is a celebration of our past, present, and future, and a tribute to our fans and customers who have supported us for decades." The announcement of the new Nissan GT-R R34 limited edition has already generated excitement among car enthusiasts and collectors, who have praised the car's blend of classic and modern features, as well as its exclusivity and rarity. Some have even compared it to other iconic limited-edition sports cars such as the Porsche 911 GT2 RS and the Lamborghini Aventador SVJ. The Nissan GT-R R34 limited edition is a testament to the enduring appeal of the GT-R nameplate, which has been associated with high performance, innovation, and passion for more than half a century. With this new model, Nissan aims to continue its legacy and inspire new generations of GT-R fans and drivers around the world.
